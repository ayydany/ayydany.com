Metroid Dread - Review

ayydany
10/03/2024
review

Synopsis

Start Date: 02/02/2021
Finished Date: 12/02/2024
Completion Date: 02/03/2024
Completion Difficulty: 3/10
Overall Ranking: 8

Okay, so I’ve just finished Metroid Dread, and what a thrill it was.

To start off, let me quickly explain my history with the game. I have sort of played the game twice. The first time I played it until about 60% done, but then i kind of lost the save file. This was around at 2021. So when I got back into it, I had to play it from the start again, but since it has passed so long, so I didn’t really feel like I was replaying the same things over again, even if I remembered some progression spots and issues.

This was because at the time I was playing around with my switch and the hacking scene, so when doing an update to it, I lost all the data it had. big oops.

Anyway, with that sorted, let’s get back to the review and what it felt like.

Some Backstory

I’m not a Metroid fan. I know about the games and I consider myself a metroidvania fan (having played quite a lot of them, like Hollow Knight), but never really played a Metroid game, neither 2D nor 3D to be fair. This was actually my first foray into Metroid on a hands-on view, and it was quite fun! I feel like the game had a superb and rewarding progression curve, which is essential in these sorts of games.

Playing the Game

Okay, so this first play through was a while ago, so don’t really recall much, but I do remember being hooked into it, playing it mostly on my free time.

At the start of the game, you’re forced into loosing all your powers, during the initial encounter. You do survive, but are thrown into the abyss of ZDR in Artaria which is the bottom area in the map. This brings me to talk about the visuals, which are very beautiful, and it’s a perfect match for the Nintendo Switch’s hardware. While exploring the are we find the remnants of the Chozo which we’re the previous habitants of ZDR, and while they do seem extinct, there might be some suprises down the road.

While progressing through the game, as a classic Metroidvania you will find some areas apparently inaccessible to you, and being as completionist as I am I would always try to find a way to break in into them, but it was mostly a wasted effort. I had to learn to just leave a mental note about them and moving on. But this brings another point of which is progression. You will find upgrades and the whole game is pretty much a go to A and B and C and etc to get all the needed upgrades to unlock something to be able to go to the next section. Sometimes you have to beat a boss as a progression checkpoint, but for most of your journey it will always be related to a upgrade you we’re missing.

At the start of the game you will find your mobility very lacking, but by the end the difference will be night and day! It will feel excellent by the end, when you can pretty much power smash through everything and not have to worry about most enemies.

Going back into the progression, the game is split into 8 major areas, I already mentioned Artarias, but there is also Dairon, Cataris, Buremia, Ghavoram, Ferenia, Elum and finally Hamubia. Most of these have a specific gimmick or purpose in the role of the Chozo society and to the benefit of ZDR. There’s also Itorash, but that’s so small, no point in counting it.

As this review is supposed to be spoiler free, I will not talk about the ending or the final boss, but it was a nice fight, while not reinventing anything, it makes you use most of your arsenal which you have built on until it, so it makes for a nice test of your abilities.

Post-game Activities

Post game cleanup was a breeze! I had to say, while I barely used Shinespark during the game, it is all over the place for the secret collectables. Almost every of the most hidden ones required a bit of precision in, charging it up somewhere around the zone, and then keeping it and chaining it together to be able to reach the collectable. There is a puzzle element to these, which is really nice and fun to see. I have to admit, thought most of the tougher ones I just ended going over on YouTube and got the solution there.

With that said, it took me about 6 hours to 100% the map, while using an online map and some additional help with the tougher collectables. If I didn’t, it would’ve taken me a bit more I’d assume. The reward for completing the game is some gallery pictures which while underwhelming, are nice to see.

Recommendation

Absolutely. It is a very fun and rewarding experience. I’d probably be happy to play it again in a couple of months (as I almost did). 100% is also really straightforward so if you’re looking into that as well.

A solid 8/10 in my book.
